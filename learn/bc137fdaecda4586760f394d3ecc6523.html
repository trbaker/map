Middle School Map Activity

World Climate Regions

Use map evidence to describe patterns, ask questions, and make a supported claim.

ArcGIS Item ID: bc137fdaecda4586760f394d3ecc6523

Activity Overview

In this short activity, students explore one digital map and answer five short-answer questions. The goal is to practice observing geographic patterns and supporting ideas with map evidence.

Suggested time: 15–20 minutes

Student Directions

  1. Open the map or data layer your teacher assigns.
  2. Zoom out to see the full pattern, then zoom in to inspect details.
  3. Click features or places on the map to look for pop-up information.
  4. Use specific map evidence in each response.

Short Answer Questions

  1. What is the main topic or pattern shown in the World Climate Regions map?
  2. Identify one place on the map that stands out. What evidence from the map supports your choice?
  3. Describe one pattern, cluster, gap, or contrast you notice.
  4. What might explain the pattern you observed in world Climate Regions?
  5. What additional map layer or data source would help you investigate this topic further?

Exit Ticket

Claim: One important pattern I noticed is...

Evidence: The map supports this because...

Question: One question I still have is...

Created for classroom use. Students should support answers with evidence from the assigned map.
