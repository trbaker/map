Exploring Earth's Biomes with a Digital Map

Objective

Use the digital biome map to investigate different land cover regions around the world and answer questions about Earth's major biomes.

Directions

  1. Open the digital biome map provided by your teacher.
  2. Zoom in and out to explore different parts of the world.
  3. Use the map legend to identify biome types.
  4. Answer the questions below using evidence from the map.

Questions

1. Boreal Forests (Taiga)

  • Locate a large area of Boreal Forests/Taiga on the map.
  • Name one country where this biome is common.
  • Why do you think this biome is found in northern latitudes?

2. Deserts

  • Find the Sahara Desert in Africa or the Gobi Desert in Asia.
  • Which desert did you examine?
  • Describe two characteristics of desert biomes.

3. Tropical Forests

  • Locate a Tropical and Subtropical Moist Broadleaf Forest.
  • Name one country where this biome is found.
  • Why does this biome support so much plant growth?

4. Grasslands and Savannas

  • Find an area of Tropical and Subtropical Grasslands, Savannas, and Shrublands in Africa.
  • Why are large forests less common in this biome?
  • Name one animal that might live there.

5. Biome Boundaries

  • Locate an area where two different biomes meet.
  • What are the two biomes?
  • Scientists call transition zones between biomes ecotones. Why might ecotones contain a mixture of plants and animals from both biomes?

Challenge Question

Which biome would you most like to visit? Use at least two observations from the map to explain your choice.

This activity should take most middle school students about 10–15 minutes and encourages both map-reading and scientific reasoning skills.
